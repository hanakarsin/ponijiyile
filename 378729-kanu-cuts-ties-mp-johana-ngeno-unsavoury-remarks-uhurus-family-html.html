KANU cuts ties with MP Johana Ng'eno after unsavoury remarks against Uhuru's family

 Posted on February 28, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  665 words  |  Enda Jantzen

- KANU party has asked Emurua Dikirr MP Johana Ng'eno to resign or he will be forced out of the party

- Secretary Nick Salat said Ng'eno's slur on Mama Ngina was tantamount to disrespect to founding president Jomo Kenyatta and President Uhuru Kenyatta

- The two leaders, Salat said, served as KANU chiefs and as such, disrespecting them was an insult to KANU

- Ng'eno was on Tuesday, charged with incitement following his arrest at his home on Monday, September 7

- The MP went bare knuckles on President Uhuru and asked him to prepare to retire to his Gatundu home adding that he was running Kenya like a property of Kenyatta's family

The Kenya African National Union party (KANU) has announced that it has expelled Emurua Dikirr MP Johana Ng'eno.

Party Secretary-General Nick Salat who addressed a press conference on the evening of Tuesday, September 8, said KANU was displeased by utterances made by Ng'eno directed to President Uhuru Kenyatta and his family.

Detestable remarks

Salat also cited alleged hate speech remarks that let to his arrest on Monday, September 7, as one of the reasons it used to resolve to sever links with the lawmaker.

"We in KANU have found that honourable Ng'eno has fallen short of provisions of Article six of the constitution and he should therefore be stripped off the privileges to serve Kenya as a state officer. In this regard, in as much as we sympathise with the people of his constituency, public interest demands that he does not continue being a member of KANU.The people of Emurua Dikirr will have to choose between peace and anarchy. The way he has been behaving is unbecoming, we, therefore, have to part ways. We are parting ways as a party and he has the freedom to pick any other party he wants but as KANU, we are parting ways with him," said Salat.

The SG observed that both President Uhuru Kenyatta and Kenya's founding president Jomo Kenyatta had served as chief of KANU and as such, disrespecting them was tantamount to insults on the independence party.

"It must not be lost to Ng’eno that both Mzee Jomo Kenyatta and President Uhuru Kenyatta are distinguished former chairmen of KANU. Therefore, to insult former leaders of KANU is to insult the party itself," Salat stated.

Ng'eno arrested

In a separate story, TUKO.co.ke reported that Ng'eno had been arrested and arraigned following alleged hate speech remarks he made over the weekend.

According to the charge sheet, the MP threatened to cause ethnic animosity after claiming 95% of the Mau Forest belonged to Kalenjin land while the Maasai only owned 5%.

In a viral video clip, the MP was captured telling the president to prepare to exit office in 2022 just like his predecessor Mwai Kibaki did and to resign is he did not want to work with his deputy William Ruto.

He also told the president to run the country knowing that it belonged to all Kenyans and not himself or his mother Mama Ngina.

When he was arraigned on Tuesday, Ng'eno pleaded not guilty to incitement and he is set to spend another night in police cells pending the hearing of his bail application.
