Garissa: Police officer stabs colleague over 'missing' gun

 Posted on March 29, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  409 words  |  Enda Jantzen

- Milton Lomuk who was on night shift went to the house of one Stephen Irungu on the morning of January 1, 2020, and left him with his gun

- He came back moments later and demanded his firearm back but Irungu had taken it to the armoury

- Lomuk became violent and attacked his colleague with a kitchen knife, leaving him with deep cuts on his left wrist

A police officer in Garissa is nursing injuries after his colleague assaulted him using a kitchen knife following a quarrel over the whereabouts of a gun.

It was reported that Milton Lomuk attacked his colleague Stephen Irungu Mwangi at his residence located about two kilometres from their work station after the latter failed to return the gun the former had given him.

According to a police statement released on Wednesday, January 1, Lomuk, who was on night shift went to Irungu's house while drunk and left him with his Ceska S/No G 5890 gun.

Irungu then decided to hand over the gun to one PC Kemboi, the armourer.

At around 10:30am on the same day, Lomuk came back and demanded his gun but was asked to collect it from the armoury

"The reportee (Irungu) later called PC Kemboi the armourer and handed him the fire arm.The accused person later came and demanded for his fire arm,he was informed that it has been taken to armoury but he became violent and attacked the reportee," added the statement.

He got violent and attacked Irungu with a kitchen knife leaving him with deep cuts on the left wrist.

In a bid to save his life, the injured officer handed over his gun to Lomuk who reportedly corked it and attempted to pull a trigger on his colleague.

Irungu, however, managed to bring the violent officer down and appealed for help from colleagues.

The Deputy OCS and PC Kemboi rushed to the scene and disarmed Lomuk.
