50s hairstyles: Easy ideas for long and short hair

 Posted on April 12, 2024  |  7 minutes  |  1479 words  |  Chauncey Koziol

Back in the 1950s, women either rocked short, medium, or long hair. With the difference in length, texture, and styling preferences of most ladies, a lot of hairstyles were introduced. The 50s hairstyles were quite intriguing because they fit various ladies' personalities. There were looks that better-suited housewives, women with long hair, and the typical chic woman.

The 1950s goes down in female hairstyle history as the year that saw class, vintage, and timeless designs that are still relevant to every fashionista. In 2020, the same hairstyles are still vibrant among runways, red-carpets, movies, and social media fashion. Let us explore some of the intriguing styles the 50s had to offer.

1950s hairstyles for short hair

The 1950 hairdo was perfect for women with short hair. Here are some of the 1950's hairstyles for short hair:

Pixie cut

One of the individuals who was known for rocking the pixie cut was Audrey Hepburn. It matched her face features perfectly, making most women desire to get the hairdo. However, it received some backlash from the media with many claiming that this was more of a men's look. However, women still rocked it, and it eventually became a 50's trademark for ladies looking for a high fashion look.

Faux bob

The faux bob was a trendy 50s look. To achieve the look, one ought to use a blow dryer and a huge round brush to get the wave. One must keep the wave big and dramatic to achieve the 50's hairstyles. After creating the wave, one should fold and pin the hair ends to get that faux bob.

The poodle cut

The poodle cut was a famous 1950s hairstyles for short hair. It became a viral 50s style hair when popular actresses such as Faye Emerson, Lucille Ball, and Peggy Garner began rocking it. The look has permed curls that highly resemble a poodle's curly hair, hence the name.

The Italian cut

The Italian cut was introduced in 1953. It highly resembled poodle hair. However, it was a bit longer than the poodle. The Italian cut was characterized by tousled curls that appeared to be spit to show more of the face and neck features. Most people saw the look as shaggy yet sophisticated. It was one of the most relaxed 1950s women hairstyles to maintain, making it a highly preferred look.

Short bangs

There were various ways women would style hair back in this century. However, one of the best 1950 hairstyles for short hair was the short bangs. These bangs were short, neatly made, and wavy. The top of the hair was flat, with that around the ears being very curly and fluffy.

Soft bob

The soft bob was a hairstyle that was widely embraced by redheads, brunettes, and even the blondes. It created a massive hair volume, which made most women fall in love with it. Rolled curls characterized the soft bob, with the volume on the sides, top or even sometimes flat, if at all one was styling with a hat.

The pageboy

The pageboy was mostly styled with the side part so that women could get that smooth and sophisticated look they desired. It was convenient for women with medium hair. It was quite common in this era. However, in 1960, it was modified into the flip.

The French twist

The 1950 French twist hairstyle is considered one of the most sophisticated looks of that decade. It was highly rocked by women of all ages, making it a trendy style. To make the style have more of that '50s vibe, women could add a bouffant or a shiny barrette to their French twist.

Roller hair

One of the most popular hair trends of the '50s was women sleeping with rollers to get the perfect curls. It soon became a look that was widely embraced even by young girls in colleges. The college girls would style the curls with a beret to give it more of a Parisian look.

1950s hairstyles for long hair

Although spotting a lady with long hair in this decade was rare, it does not mean that there were no looks women with such length could style. Here are some of the looks that women with long hair rocked during this era:

The Bettie page hairstyle

The Bettie Page hairstyle was a modern look in this era. It was a style that caught the eye of most ladies because it was quite the opposite of how most professional ladies would style their hair in the 1950s.

However, it was embraced in the counter-culture and ladies who would rock it in the United Kingdom were "Beat girls" and "Rebel girls" in the United States of America. With time, ladies began accessorizing the style with a bandana.

The Hollywood pin-up

By the 1950s, women hairstyle trends were promoted by Hollywood pin-up models such as Marilyn Monroe. They would either have all their hair in curls or hold part of it in a sleek bun, letting curls lay in one side of their head. The one side with curls made this look very stylish.

Horsetail /ponytail

The ponytail, also referred to as the horsetail, was a favorite 50s hairdo look among most young girls and women. It was effortless to maintain, straightforward to style, simple, yet high fashion. Celebrities such as Bridgette Bardot were occasionally spotted rocking this chic look.

The style entailed pulling the hair back to the center of their head and securing it tightly with a band. Some made the look more stylish by using a headband to accessorize the ponytail.

Bouffant

The bouffant was created in 1950, and by 1960, it had gained the fashion sense of famous people such as the First Lady Betty Ford. The main characteristic of the bouffant was the massive volume and height. A lot of hairsprays were used to maintain their steady look. One could not wear a hat with the bouffant. However, one could accessorize the bouffant with feathers, bows, or some jewels.

How do you style a bouffant?

Since the Bouffant is all about hair mass, freshly wash your hair for easy expansion and elasticity. Secondly, put on a tight headband as you push the mane 2 inches upward and away from your forehead.

Proceed to tease your hair using a comb towards your scalp. Tease it from the ends to the scalp. The next step is to streamline the hair by using a comb. A hair spray of choice will make sure that the hair remains in a bouffant state the entire day.

How do you style your hair with a headband?

In the ’50s, ladies used to accessorize their hair with things such as the bandanna and the headbands. While styling with a headband, the ladies first had to hold their hair into a ponytail or a horsetail. After that, one would add the headband to make a more stylish look.

What is a victory roll hairstyle?

The victory rolls were among the most popular, stylish, and versatile hairstyles of 1940. They were loved because women could manipulate them for both formal and casual styling. The style entailed women styling their hair into two Victory Rolls. After that, one had to add a headscarf or pin the rest of their hair up.
