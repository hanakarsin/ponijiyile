I can't ignore my opponents, they're in Kenya to stay - Uhuru

 Posted on April 21, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  464 words  |  Enda Jantzen

- President Uhuru Kenyatta said he would not discriminate against Kenyans who did not vote for him

- The Head of State wondered why some leaders within his circle were against his cooperation with his perceived opponents

- He said no one was leaving Kenya because they did not support his presidency and he had an obligation to serve them and ensure the country was united.

President Uhuru Kenyatta has reiterated his commitment to work with the Opposition and its supporters saying they are equally Kenyans like those who voted for his reelection.

The Head of State was categorical he could not ignore working with leaders and a significant percentage of Kenyans who did not support his presidency because his priority was to unite the country.

Speaking on Friday, March 29, Uhuru hit out at Jubilee leaders who were against his cooperation with politicians from the Opposition camp, saying it was impractical since they were also Kenyans.

He emphasised the importance of tolerance and unity, citing the true attributes as key pillars on which the war against runaway corruption could be built.

"There is around 48% of voters who did not vote for me but I must work with them because they are Kenyans too," said the president.

Uhuru wondered where those who were against his cooperation with the Opposition wanted the latter to go.

Notably, Jubilee politicians allied to Deputy President William Ruto have incessantly opposed Uhuru's handshake with Opposition leader Raila Odinga. terming it a fraud and a plot by te latter to frustrate their 2022 preferred presidential candidate's ambition.

"One time a journalist asked former Zimbabwe President Robert Mugabe when he would let the Zimbabweans go. He replied asking where the citizens were going. The same way if you ask me to let those who did not vote for me to go, I wonder where they are supposed to go. They are Kenyans and deserve to be served," quipped the president.

He was clear that nobody would reverse his stance to work with his opponents, saying the move was in the best interest of about 50 million Kenyans.

Uhuru blasted leaders politicising his unity pact with the Opposition and the war against graft in funerals and weddings over weekends, challenging them to find something constructive to do.
