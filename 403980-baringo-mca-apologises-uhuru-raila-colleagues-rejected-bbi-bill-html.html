Baringo MCA apologises to Uhuru, Raila after colleagues rejected BBI bill

The leader of the minority at the Baringo County Assembly Sam Lourien has said the region remains solidly behind the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI).

The Tirioko MCA said his colleagues who shot down the BBI bill acted out of political influence from unnamed individuals and due to lack of knowledge in terms of what citizens stand to gain.

Speaking to journalists on Wednesday, February 24, Lourien asked President Uhuru Kenyatta and ODM leader Raila Odinga to forgive the county assembly over their BBI vote.

"I want to apologise on behalf of MCAs who voted no. They did so out of ignorance. I want to ask Raila and President Uhuru to forgive us and continue with the ongoing road projects in the region," he said.

The ward representative claimed the bill was misconstrued to mean a contest between Deputy President William Ruto and Baringo senator Gideon Moi.

He observed that Gideon could still become president if he contested in 2022 without votes of people from his backyard; thus, the MCAs' BBI vote was inconsequential.

"The BBI was a contest between Gideon Moi and Ruto, but people should remember former president Moi won presidential election with votes from other regions. He could get few votes here," he added.

The county became the first county to reject the BBI bill when its 30 members voted overwhelmingly to reject the Constitution Bill, 2020.

Only five MCAs voted in support of the bill.

The MCAs had made their positions regarding the motion known even before the debate kicked off, and when the motion was moved, it snowballed into a shouting match.

TUKO.co.ke previously reported on the chaos and commotion that rocked the assembly as KANU MCAs and Jubilee colleagues traded barbs as the minority demanded public participation.

“Mr Speaker, I know there are some Jubilee Party members here who are conniving to shoot down the bill which the government has sponsored. This is very unfair,” said Churo Amaya MCA Ameja Selemoi.

As of Wednesday, February 24, at least 41 out 42 county assemblies which debated the bill had approved it.
