He knew too well that he could not risk putting all his eggs in one basket and decided to venture into business while he focussed on acting.

The talented thespian had a sit down with Churchill and detailed how his entrepreneurial skills helped him grow an empire he is completely proud of.

According to the father, husband and artiste, he knows the best way to develop his empire is with his woman by his side.

He did not always want to be an actor and would always slack when it came to rehearsals.

"I did not want to be an actor. I was more focussed in business," Njoro said.

The hardworking man revealed he runs a few matatus, owns bars, has rental houses and also owns a hardware.

His secret is delegating duties and the thespian's wife handles most of the ventures.

"I let my wife deal with the businesses then she brings the books at the end of the month," he added.

The 41-year-old also tries to be cordial with police officers who always offer their help whenever it is needed.

"Sometimes they will ask you not to mess with Njoro. Even when they need me, I try my best to help them," he explained.
