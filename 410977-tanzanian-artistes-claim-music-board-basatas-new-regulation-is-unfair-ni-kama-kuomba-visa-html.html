Tanzanian Artistes Claim Music Board Basata's New Regulation Is Unfair: &quot;Ni Kama Kuomba Visa&quot;

 Posted on May 13, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  538 words  |  Enda Jantzen

- A new regulation has been issued in Tanzania that will require artistes to submit their new songs for approval before release

- Tanzanian musicians have come out to strongly criticise the move saying that it will stall and kill art

- Fans have also come out to support the artistes saying that the move will kill creativity and slow down the pace of artistes releasing music

Tanzanian artistes are raising concerns concerning the music governing body BASATA's new regulations to approve music before it gets airplay.

The Baraza La Sanna Ya Taifa board is responsible for giving artistes the go-ahead to get their songs played on radio and TV stations in Tanzania. Now, the musicians feel like it is stifling their craft.

Earlier, Wasafi TV, owned by renowned singer Diamond Plutnumz posted a long message on Instagram criticising the board.

Part of their long post read:

"Looks like there is still a big issue involving artistes sending their new songs to BASATA for approval before they are released.The artistes are now starting to talk about this with most disagreeing with this move and sharing their opinions on traditional and social media."

The Habari Ndio Hiyo singer MwanaFa also shared his thought concerning the new censorship move that requires artistes to submit their songs before they are released.

He also shared a long thread on Instagram, explaining why he is against this move, adding that there are new ways the board can use to stop 'dirty' songs from getting airplay.

Part of his in-depth message read:

"If BASATA and COSOTA want to implement all laws, they should start with the event law issued in 1999 prohibiting places from using artiste's music without a license. Many artistes have lost lots of money because of this.
