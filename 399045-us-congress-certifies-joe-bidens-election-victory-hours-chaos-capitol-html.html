The US Congress has affirmed the Electoral College win of President-elect Joe Biden over President Donald Trump.

Biden and Vice President-elect Kamala Harris surpassed the 270-threshold required to win the presidency by counting Vermont's three electoral votes.

The Senate and House ignored objections to tossing out the electoral votes of Georgia and Pennsylvania for Biden.

Republicans also objected to Arizona, Nevada and Michigan's electoral votes, but the motions failed before they reached debate.

The qualification comes after pro-Trump rioters invaded the US Capitol halting, for several hours, the joint session of Congress, which is normally a ceremonial step.

Proceedings resumed later on with Vice President Mike Pence, bringing the Senate session back into order.

“The announcement of the state of the vote by the President of the Senate shall be deemed a sufficient declaration of the persons elected President and Vice President of the United States," Pence said following the count of all the Electoral College votes."...Each for the term beginning on January 20, 2021, and shall be entered together with the list of the votes on the journals of the Senate and the House of Representatives," he added.

Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell, R-Ky. said the nation's government faced greater challenges than what he referred to as an "unhinged crowd" while reconvening the Senate after order was restored.
