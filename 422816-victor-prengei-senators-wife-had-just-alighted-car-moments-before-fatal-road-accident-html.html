Victor Prengei: Senator's Wife Had Just Alighted from Car Moments Before Fatal Road Accident

 Posted on May 13, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  521 words  |  Lourie Helzer
  • Nominated Senator Victor Prengei's wife had just alighted from the car at Nakuru before the tragic accident
  • Prengei and Catherine were travelling together home when she opted to alight at Nakuru and get another car so as to get home ahead of her husband
  • However, as soon as she arrived home, she was informed of her husband's accident which claimed his life

Nominated senator Victor Prengei who died in a grisly road crash a few days ago had just dropped his wife Catherine in Nakuru moments before the accident that claimed his life.

The lawmaker passed away on Monday, August 16, after a fatal road accident along the Nakuru-Kabarak highway.

A report by the Daily Nation indicated on the fateful evening, the couple was from their Marioshoni farm in Eastern Mau and were on their way home in Mercy Njeri.

On arrival at Nakuru, Catherine opted to take another vehicle to get home ahead of her husband.

No sooner had she arrived home than she received a disturbing call informing her that her husband had been involved in a road accident.

"The two were together in Nakuru town, but his wife decided to go home earlier, leaving him behind. On arriving, someone called and informed her that he had been involved in an accident and that he had been taken to War Memorial Hospital,” a family source, Daniel Kobei said.

Last respects

Family and friends gathered at the Umash Funeral Home on Tuesday, August 17, to pay their last respect to Prengei who was the only lawmaker from the Ogiek community.

According to Nakuru County Police Commander Beatrice Kiraguri, the accident occurred at around 9.45pm on Monday at Gioto, along the Nakuru, Kabarak Highway.

She said the senator was driving at a high speed when he lost control of the car, veered off the road and hit a wall.

“As a result of the impact, the driver, Mr Prengei, sustained injuries and was rushed to Nakuru War Memorial Hospital but succumbed to injuries while receiving treatment,” she said.

Last encounter

On his part, his elder brother Joseph Saitoti said he was still struggling to come to terms with the news of his demise as they had spent Sunday, August 15, together.

Saitoti said they both shared their plans with the lawmaker telling him his plans for his family and Ogiek community.

The sad news of his death was received with shock as Kenyans and colleagues to the late Prengei took to social media to mourn.

"Rest well my colleague Hon. Victor Prengei, Nominated Sen. We shall forever cherish your tireless efforts in fighting for & protecting the rights of the minority communities. We have lost a great leader. My condolences to your family, friends & the entire Ogiek community," tweeted Millicent Omanga.

President Uhuru Kenyatta, Deputy President William Ruto and Raila Odinga also sent their condolence messages to friends and family of the deceased.
