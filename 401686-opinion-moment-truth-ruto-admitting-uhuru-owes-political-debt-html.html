Opinion: Moment of truth for Ruto after admitting Uhuru owes him no political debt

Deputy President William Ruto must now walk the talk following his public admission that President Uhuru Kenyatta does not owe him political support with regard to the 2022 presidential election.

For several years now, Ruto has made ‘debt payment’ his political strategy as he tirelessly campaigns for the presidency.

His henchmen, especially politicians from Mt Kenya and Rift Valley regions, have been shouting to anyone who cares to listen that Uhuru owes Ruto support in 2022; demanding that the pact be honoured.

This sense of entitlement has seen the DP raid the president’s Mt Kenya backyard to get the message home; with his allies even boldly warning the natives of ugly outcomes if they don’t play by the rules.

The threats intensified after Uhuru and ODM leader Raila Odinga entered the 2018 handshake deal. Ruto and his allies saw the gesture as a weapon waged against his presidential ambitions in favour of Raila.

Before Ruto came out to clear the air in Trans Mara last weekend, a day after the president categorically made it clear in Sagana that he did not owe anyone a debt, the issue had become too emotive that it was threatening to reverse the gains made since 2013 to foster peace in the Rift Valley, the region that was affected the most by the bloody 2007/08 post-election violence.

Indeed, the deadly violence and the subsequent arraignment of Uhuru and Ruto as well as then Industrialisation minister Henry Kosgey, head of public service Francis Muthaura, former police commissioner Hussein Ali and radio journalist Joshua arap Sang at the International Criminal Court at The Hague was the reason they came together under the Jubilee Alliance.

While the cases against the two and Sang went to full trial before collapsing, their unity continued and they won re-election in 2007 before schisms developed after the DP launched premature campaigns.

Their mission was to ensure that the two communities would never again resort to violence on account of political disputes.

While Ruto deserves credit for finally putting the false ‘debt’ narrative to rest, he must now walk the talk.

The DP must prove to Kenyans that his change of tune is not for political convenience. This is because he has perfected the art of speaking from both sides of the mouth.

For instance, he talks of plans to dedicate himself in helping the president to unite and develop the country, yet what he has been doing on the campaign trail is to undermine what the government is doing to uplift the lives of Kenyans and working to get them addicted to tokenism.

Last weekend, Ruto did not appear bothered as Kiharu MP Ndindi Nyoro insisted that the debt must be paid before his very own eyes, even as the crowd cheered the MP on.

His word is only as credible as that of his allies, and this raised question marks on his honesty.

To prove he means his word, the DP should rein in his allies who have formed a tendency of getting out targeted messages seen to be his work.

Even more importantly, he should henceforth solicit for votes on the basis of ideology and policies rather than whipping up emotions and playing the victim card.

With the mutual admission that there exists no political debt, the Tanga Tanga group should equally allow Kenyans to make rational and independent decisions on the proposed constitutional changes at the referendum, which is planned for June 2020, without politicising the matter.

It is also my hope that the DP will rise to the occasion and tame his reckless lieutenants who have been abusing the president and his family, a disgraceful trend that has gained root in recent months.

At the weekend, former Nairobi governor Mike Sonko hurled expletives at Uhuru, which the media did well to ignore because no self-respecting publication could publish them.

While Sonko has gained notoriety for such conduct, Emurua Dikirr Johanna Ng’eno and his Kapseret counterpart Oscar Sudi have in the past also attacked the First Family using obscenities.

While Uhuru has shown remarkable restraint in the face of the vulgar attacks, owe unto those who will be caught in the lights when Uhuru finally declares enough is enough and unleashes his wrath.
