Kenya Among African Countries Set to Benefit From Googles New KSh 110b Funding

 Posted on August 9, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  491 words  |  Enda Jantzen
  • Google on Wednesday launched the African Investment Fund initiative to support startups in their growth and also recover from economic shocks of COVID-19
  • The fund will invest KSh 5.5b in startups, and provide them with access to its employees, network and technologies to help them build meaningful products for their communities
  • In Kenya, Google will partner with Safaricom to support the launch of a device financing plan aimed at deepening access to smart technology and faster internet

Search engine giant Google has disclosed it will invest to the tune of KSh 110.6 billion in African counties including Kenya over the next five years to support digital transformation.

The technology giant said the funding will ensure access to fast and cheaper internet, as well as support startups and entrepreneurs, who are the lifeline of the African economy.

The announcement was made during a virtual event, where the Africa Investment Fund was also launched.

As part of the funding, Google said it would use the newly launched Fund to invest KSh 5.5 billion in startups, and provide them with access to its employees, network and technologies to help them build meaningful products for their communities.

KSh 1.1 billion recovery from COVID-19 fund

At the same time, Google said it would collaborate with not-for-profit organisation Kiva, to provide KSh 1.1 billion in low-interest loans to help small businesses and entrepreneurs in Kenya, Ghana, Nigeria and South Africa. This is to help them recover from the negative impact of the coronavirus pandemic.

The investment will also include the landing of the subsea cable Equiano, which will enable faster internet speeds and lower connectivity costs.

“The subsea cable Equiano will go through South Africa, Namibia, Nigeria and St Helena and connect the continent with Europe,” the company said.

Google also said that it has collaborated with Kenya’s largest carrier Safaricom to support the launch of a device financing plan in Kenya.

The firm said it envisions expanding this initiative across Africa with partners including Airtel, M-KOPA, MTN, Orange, Transsion Holdings and Vodacom among others.

African startups

Commenting on the investment, Google and Alphabet CEO Sundar Pichai said that despite huge strides being made in the last decade, there remains more work to do to make the internet accessible, affordable and useful for every African.

At the same time, Nitin Gajria, the Managing Director for Google in Africa said there had been more investment rounds into tech startups in the last year than ever before

“I am of the firm belief that no one is better placed to solve Africa’s biggest problems than Africa’s young developers and startup founders. We look forward to deepening our partnership with, and support for, Africa’s innovators and entrepreneurs,” Gajria said.
