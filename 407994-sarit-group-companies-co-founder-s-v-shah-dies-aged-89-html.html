Sarit Group of Companies Co-Founder S V. Shah Dies Aged 89

 Posted on January 1, 0001  |  3 minutes  |  452 words  |  Hettie Henneman

- Sarit Centre co-founder and business tycoon Sobhagayachand Vidhu Shah passed away on Tuesday, April 6

- The sad news was confirmed by the company itself, which said the late Shah passed away while receiving treatment

- The tycoon, who is also the founder of Text Book Centre, was remembered by the centre for his contribution to making it the company that it is today

Business tycoon and co-founder of the Sarit Centre Sobhagayachand Vidhu Shah has died at the age of 86.

The businessman who owned, among other investments, the Sarit Center in Westlands, Nairobi county, died on Tuesday, April 6, evening while receiving treatment at a hospital in Nairobi.

His death was announced by Sarit Group of Companies which honoured him for his contributions in helping the company grow into what it is today.

It was, however, not immediately clear what the businessman was ailing from.

President Uhuru Kenyatta paid tribute to the late businessman, describing him as Kenya's best and most innovative leader whose business acumen contributed greatly to the country’s economy.

"Mzee Bachubhai was a true patriot and a nationalist who created job opportunities for many Kenyans through his entrepreneurial exploits," Uhuru said. "He strongly and truly believed, and lived the Kenyan dream of hard work, determination and success."

The head of state further noted that his entrepreneurial spirit saw him establish Sarit Centre, Kenya's first shopping mall in the 1980s when nobody else thought about it.

Uhuru further noted that Shah moved on to establish other successful businesses, including Text Book Centre, which has continued to quench Kenyans thirst for knowledge.

He also remembered Shah as a determined philanthropist who teamed up with other well-wishers to set up Cancer Care Kenya.

This was an initiative that supports government's efforts to provide affordable cancer treatment in the country.

"As a country, we will always be grateful for Shah's generous contributions in the education and health sectors. Using his own resources, he set up a school in Murang’a County, which has continued to be a centre of excellence," said Uhuru.

According to the preisdent, the business tycoon has left a rich and lasting legacy of achievement.
