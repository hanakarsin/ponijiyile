Kenya Records 25 COVID-19 Deaths in the Last 24 Hours as Third Wave Intensifies

 Posted on May 20, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  523 words  |  Hettie Henneman

- The Ministry of Health said health facilities were experiencing more cases of infections that are widespread among communities

- According to the ministry, the number of those seeking hospitalisation because of complications associated with COVID-19 had shot up

- The country recorded 25 deaths while 1,127 others tested positive for the disease within the last 24 hours

The Ministry of Health (MoH), on Tuesday, March 23, confirmed 25 more people had lost their lives to COVID-19, the second highest-ever number of deaths from the virus within 24 hours.

Health Chief Administrative Secretary (CAS) Rashid Aman said the deaths now bring the total number of fatalities since the virus was reported in the country in March 2020 to 2,048.

The ministry also confirmed 1,127 COVID-19 infections from a sample size of 5,393 tested for the virus in the last 24 hours.

"Three occurred in the last 24 hours while 22 are late death reports from facility record audits," the ministry said in a tweet. "Our sincere condolences to the families and friends of those who have lost their loved ones," it added.

The total number of confirmed cases now stands at 123,167.

On the positive side, 210 patients recovered from the disease out of whom 100 came from various health facilities while 110 were discharged from home-based care.

The total recoveries increased to 90,586.

"We thank our healthcare workers for the good job they are doing to save the lives of our people," said MoH.

Out of those who tested positive for the disease, 1,090 patients are currently admitted to various health facilities countrywide, while 3,224 patients are on Home Based Isolation and Care.

One hundred and thirty-five (135) patients are admitted to the Intensive Care Unit (ICU), 34 of whom are on ventilatory support and 93 on supplemental oxygen.

The ministry said eight patients were on observation.

Fifty-five patients are separately on supplementary oxygen, with 42 of them in the general wards and 13 in the High Dependency Unit (HDU).

According to the ministry, the number of those seeking hospitalisation in public and private health facilities resulting from complications associated with the COVID-19 had in recent weeks shot up.

"This means we are experiencing more cases of infections that are widespread among our communities," said MoH.
