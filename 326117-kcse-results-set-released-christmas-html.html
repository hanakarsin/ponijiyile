KCSE results set to be released before Christmas

 Posted on April 25, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  346 words  |  Chauncey Koziol

- At least 700,000 candidates sat for the 2019 KCSE exams

- Education CS George Magoha said marking of the exams will begin next week

- There were few cases of exam cheating in various parts of the country

The 2019 Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) results are set to be released before Wednesday, December 25.

The announcement was made by Education Cabinet Secretary George Magoha on Friday, November 22.

The CS was speaking at Kapsisiywa Secondary School in Nandi county where he delivered a KSh 100 million cheque from President Uhuru Kenyatta for the construction of Eliud Kipchoge Library in honour of the legendary athlete.

"I would like to assure the public that we shall go to Christmas after we have known how the children have performed, so that we can again plan well for the future," Magoha said in a video by NTV seen by TUKO.co.ke.

Magoha said marking of the exams will begin next week and will be done in good time to ensure parents have enough time to prepare their kids for life after high school.

At least 700,000 candidates sat for the 2019 KCSE that was witnessed by a few cases of exam cheating in various parts of the country.

At least 26 candidates were arrested in the capital Nairobi on Wednesday, November 6, over alleged exam malpractices.

The students were believed to have been receiving exam answers in real time via their mobile phones from unknown sources.

In Kisii county, 16 KCSE candidates at Milimani Ramasha Academy failed to show up for exams after 11 impersonators were arrested.
