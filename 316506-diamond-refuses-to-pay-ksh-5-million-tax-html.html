Diamond at loggerheads with Tanzania Revenue Authority over KSh 5 million tax

 Posted on March 8, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  348 words  |  Chauncey Koziol

Tanzania's creme de la creme musician Diamond Platinumz has picked issue with the country's taxman for trying to allegedly overtax his company.

The singer recently faced Tanzania Revenue Authority (TRA) staff and complained about their move to tax him over KSh 5 million for holding his show in one of the country's provinces.

Speaking with the TRA team at their stand during a tourism expo in Shinyanga, the star put the taxman on the spot claiming he was asked to pay an arm and a leg to plaster Wasafi Festival posters across Iringa town, where it was previously held.

The singer openly revealed he refused to pay the tax as he felt he was being robbed in broad daylight.

According to the star, the tax and charges exceeded what his team was going to make at the end of the show hence the decision to ''evade'' them.

Diamond wondered why the government has been trying to oppress Wasafi despite the company duly paying its annual taxes and abiding by all laws.

''For instance, Wasafi Limited usually pay its taxes at the end of every financial year. Among the things we do and pay for include the Wasafi Festivals. It is not fair for us to pay the required 18% tax at the end of every year and still pay taxes at the end of every show organised by Wasafi,'' he said.

The TRA officers advised Diamond to always preserve all the receipts and tax documents so as to present to its staff whenever they come knocking.
