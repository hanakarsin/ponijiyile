Nollywood actor Rexperfect has advised the youth to be content with whatever they have.

In an exclusive interview with TUKO.co.ke, the actor who is also a radio presenter dissuaded the youth from trying to imitate other people’s lifestyles.

“Contentment is the way up. The youths must learn to cut their coats according to their size. Living beyond your means to fit in is a recipe for disaster. Create a life that feels good on the inside, not one that looks good on the outside. Know what you want, go for it but never be desperate because desperation kills the vision,” he said.

Who is Rexperfect?

Rexperfect Chinedu Agoawuike, popularly known as The Entertainment Governor, wears many hats. He is an actor and also a social activist.

First son in a family of 8, the movie star hails from the Imo state in Nigeria.

Rexperfect told TUKO.co.ke that he started acting professionally in 2017.

“I have starred in over ten movies. I started as a script supervisor before beginning my acting career,” he divulged.

Lessons from Nollywood

The Mental movie star further pointed out that Kenya and Africa can learn valuable lessons from Nollywood.

Rexperfect noted that Nollywood movies play a crucial role in informing the younger generation of their roots and culture.

“Most of the movies are historical. Younger generations get to know the roots, inherent culture and way of life. Originality is an aspect most Nigerian filmmakers try not to compromise.
