Police service dismisses reports police officer stole from colleague during Sonko's arrest

 Posted on February 20, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  384 words  |  Lourie Helzer

- On Friday, December 6, police in Voi arrested Nairobi Governor Mike Sonko

- During the arrest a scuffle ensued between the police and the county boss

- It was all captured on camera and Kenyans were keen to observe the video

- Their attention was drawn to one police officer who seemed to be engaging in other activities during the scuffle

- Police have since dismissed the video saying it was not what many Kenyans thought it was

The National Police Service (NPS) has dismissed reports that a police officer pick-pocketed his senior colleague during the dramatic arrest of Nairobi governor, Mike Sonko, in Voi.

The agency was responding to a viral video doing rounds on social media indicating a police officer grabbed something from his colleague during the scuffle that ensued at Voi airstrip on Friday, December 6.

In the video, one of the the officers is seen to have exchanged something behind the back of his colleague who then turns around to see what was going on before the two look opposite side as if nothing happened.

Hawk-eyed Kenyans took the video and studied closely with claims that the officer had stolen something from his senior.

However, the police service says the video was a misinterpretation of facts and that the officer did not steal anything.

"The video clip doing rounds on social media highlighting what appears to be an act of pick pocketing by a police officer is a misrepresentation of facts and untrue."The officer in charge of traffic Voi has confirmed that he was not pick pocketed. Equally, the police command in Voi has confirmed that the officer in question is disciplined and of high integrity," said NPS ina Twitter post on Sunday, December 8.

Nevertheless, Kenyans on social media were not ready to buy that explanation claiming they know what they saw.
