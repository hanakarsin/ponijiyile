Kibra by-election: William Ruto hits back at Rachel Shebesh for supporting ODM man

 Posted on April 16, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  431 words  |  Hettie Henneman

- The DP urged Jubilee supporters not to listen to Shebesh and other party members campaigning for their rival

- He declared Shebesh a rebel who together with others should henceforth be treated with utter contempt

- Shebesh is one of Jubilee Party members who have publicly declared support for ODM candidate in the Kibra by-election

Deputy President William Ruto has fired back at Youth and Gender Affairs Chief Administrative Secretary (CAS) Rachel Shebesh for openly endorsing ODM party candidate Imran Okoth during Kibra by-election campaigns.

Ruto was evidently irked by Shebesh's decision to campaign for Imran, instead of the flag bearer of her own party Jubilee, and declared the former Nairobi woman representative a rebel who should be ignored and treated with utmost contempt.

In a fiery tweet on Saturday, September 28, the DP also called on Jubilee members to continue giving their man Mariga, a footballer turned politician, all the support he needs during the Kibra campaigns, assuring them of sweet victory.

"All Jubilee party supporters should ignore and treat with utter contempt rebels and those rejected by the electorate who now deceptively purport to speak for the party leadership. Let's give Mariga our flag bearer moral, material and prayerful support. Victory is surely beckoning," he said.

Shebesh had on Friday declared her support for the ODM candidate, Imran, brother of late area MP Ken Okoth, during which she also alleged President Uhuru Kenyatta was coerced to endorse Mariga.

Addressing women in Kibra constituency, the ex-lawmaker claimed she was at State House when Ruto pressurised the president to give the retired footballer his 'blessing'.

“On that day when the president endorsed Mariga for Kibra race, I was in State House. I know how they mounted pressure on him, and how they gained entry to State House," she said.

The other Jubilee Pparty candidates who had also made it known they would not support Mariga for the Kibra parliamentary seat include nominated MP Maina Kamanda and his Gatundu South counterpart Moses Kuria.
