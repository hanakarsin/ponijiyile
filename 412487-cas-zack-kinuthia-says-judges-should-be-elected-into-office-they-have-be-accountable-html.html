Sports Chief Administrative Secretary Zack Kinuthia has proposed that judges from the High Court level upwards should henceforth be elected into office.

The youthful government official joined other pro-handshake leaders in criticising the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) judgment by a five-judge bench of the High Court and stated the need to introduce a formula that will hold judicial officers to account.

“Even by constitutional terms, a governor cannot go more than two terms, a Member of Parliament is deemed to have finished their term after five years even though that is perception. If we can go that route, we must ask today that even judges from the High Court level upwards also be elected by the people so that they are also accountable,” Kinuthia said.

The CAS, who was speaking during an awards ceremony for top-performing leaders by Mizani Africa on Tuesday, May 18, insisted that judges should be answerable to the people while in office.

“Presidents all over the world are shaking towards elections, MPs too...Why can’t we also hold judges accountable? If they make bad decisions, what we are going to do as a country?” he posed.

Agriculture Cabinet Secretary Peter Munya had earlier claimed the decision to declare the BBI unconstitutional was politically motivated and did not adhere to the law.

“If you listen to what they were saying, it is like they were picking words from somebody. It is as if the judgment was written somewhere... especially when you see them attacking the president as if they are politicians on a podium,” he said.

The bench appointed by retired chief justice David Maraga on Thursday, May 13, stated that the BBI process was illegitimate and a violation of the constitution.

The court unanimously declared that Uhuru overstretched his mandate by initiating a process to change the constitution.

“The president, government, or any state organ cannot be an initiator of and an umpire in amendment of the constitution,” the court ruled.

Raila calls for judges’ respect

Former prime minister Raila Odinga, a chief proponent of BBI, had earlier asked leaders across the country to desist from attacking and disparaging the judges even though he disagreed with the ruling.

“In particular, I want to urge that we restrain from personalised attacks on the court and its members. We may disagree with the court but we must respect its ruling and its freedom to exercise its judgment as it understands the legal and constitutional matters before it,” he stated.
