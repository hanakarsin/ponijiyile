Nandi: Mosop MP Vincent Tuwei secretly joins teachers' Whatsapp group, confronts them for gossiping

 Posted on May 14, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  396 words  |  Hettie Henneman

Mosop MP Vincent Tuwei surprised teachers after disclosing he had secretly joined their Whatsapp groups to confirm rumours that they were plotting to derail his service delivery.

The first time combative Jubilee MP went ahead to tell the teachers he had established individuals hell-bent on tainting his name and declared unspecified consequences upon them.

Speaking during a funeral service at Kimogoch village, Nandi county the legislator said the educators had formed camps to resist his push for transfers of non-performing head teachers.

“I have noted that I am the main topic of discussion in your Whatsapp groups. Despite few of you defending my campaign to improve education most of you are plotting evil against me. You should know I joined those Whatsapp groups and I know who have been gossiping about me,” said Tuwei.

The legislator cautioned teachers to review their rebellious position by accepting to work with him to improve performance.

He accused the teachers of identifying a National Assembly candidate to contest against him in 2022 because of his tough stand aimed enhancing performance in the constituency.

"When I genuinely question some head teachers about poor performance of their schools they overlook my concern. I am not a small boy but a mature man knowledgeable on education issues,” he said.

Tuwei said his position was powerful thus he was able to influence how teachers’ issues are handled through Parliament, explaining that the Teachers Service Commission was accountable to the National Assembly.

“I am not like the former legislators. I harbour good intentions for our children and teachers as well. The TSC boss is accountable to me as a legislator and I can influence transfer issues. I defended you during the enmasse delocolisation but if you continue gossiping about me in those Whatsapp groups I will change my stance,” the legislator stated.
