Gwen Goldman: 70-Year-Old Woman Gets Her Childhood Bat Girl Dream Job Six Decades after Being Reject

  • A 70-year-old woman requested to join a baseball team as a bat girl but her request was rejected by the then team's general manager
  • She, however, hang that rejection letter on her wall for sixty years before the current manager reached out to her and made her childhood dream come true
  • Gwen Goldman served as a bat girl during Monday 28 game between the New York Yankees and Los Angeles Angels match

A 70-year-old woman identified as Gwen Goldman has inspired people online after it emerged that she got a position she applied for 60 years ago.

Gwen Goldman's inspirational story

Gwen did not give up on her childhood dream of becoming a bat girl for the New York Yankees despite her request to serve being met with rejection more than six decades ago.

According to the People Magazine, she became an honorary bat girl during the Yankees' home game played on Monday, 28 against the Los Angeles Angels.

She also got an opportunity to throw out the ceremonial first pitch, which was part of the 2021 HOPE Week Initiative, which purposes to inspire people.

Her story was posted online on the Yankees' Twitter page.

This is what they shared online:

"In 1961, 10-year-old Gwen Goldman penned a letter to the Yankees expressing her dream of being a batgirl. The response she received from the GM at the time still hangs on her living room wall. This HOPE Week, the Yankees will make 70-year-old Gwen’s dream come true.""60 years later, in a new letter to Gwen from the current Yankees GM, Brian Cashman invites her to Yankee Stadium to fulfill her dream," continued the post.
