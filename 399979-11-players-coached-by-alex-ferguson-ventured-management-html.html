Manchester United’s record goalscorer Wayne Rooney was on Friday, January 15 confirmed as the new Derby county manager as he brought an official end to his playing career.

Tributes poured in for a better part of Friday and Saturday as fans and players congratulated Rooney on a successful playing career as they wished him the best in his new adventure as manager.

The 35-year old is however simply the latest former Manchester United player to venture into football management.

In fact, a number of players formerly coached by Sir Alex Ferguson at Manchester United took the management route, some successfully while others with horrendous results.

TUKO looks at some of Alex Ferguson’s students who turned to management and how they faired in the job.

1.Steve Bruce

Bruce, a former Manchester United centre back is currently boss at Newcastle United and has been appointed 11 times.

His longest stint as manager was with Birmingham City, a team he helped get promotion in 2007.

2.Bryan Robson

Robson’s managerial career kicked off in 1994 when he became player-manager at Middlesbrough where he led them to promotion in 1995.

He has also had stints with Bradford City, West Brom and Sheffield United but none have been as successful.

3.Roy Keane

The famous former Manchester United captain is known for his fiery personality and he transitioned to coaching after retiring from the game.

He started with Sunderland whom he helped earn promotion in 2007 but stepped down the following season after dismall results.

Keane moved to Ipswich but was again dismissed in 2011. Currently, he is an enthusiastic pundit with Sky Sports.

4.Mark Hughes

Hughes is one of the fairly successful managers who was managed by sir Alex Ferguson.

He began with the Welsh national team between1999-2004 where he nearly masterminded qualification to the Euros.

Hughes also managed Manchester City just when the club was transitioning to new wealthy owners but was sacked a year later. He has also managed the likes of Stoke City and Southampton but was sacked by both teams.

5.Ole Gunnar Solskjaer

The baby-faced assassin was Manchester United’s hero in 1999 when he scored the decisive goal as the Red Devils downed Bayern Munich in the Champions League final.

As coach, he made a dream return to Manchester United in 2018 and despite the lack of silverware success, he has an outward chance of winning the Premier League in the 2020/2021 campaign.

6.Paul Ince

The former striker began his managerial career promisingly with MK Dons in 2007-08 but was sacked after promoting the side to league One.

7.Phil Neville

A member of the class of 92, Phil first took a stab at management when he took a caretaker role at Salford City in 2015.

He however made a name for himself in management when he took over as the England women’s national team boss.

8.Gary Neville

Phil’s brother Gary was appointed Valencia manager in 2015 and had such a torrid time including suffering an embarrassing 7-0 defeat to Barcelona that he resorted to punditry.

9.Ryan Giggs

Giggs started his managerial career as interim boss at his former club Manchester United.

He is on a steady rise in his new venture and is currently the Wales national team manager.
